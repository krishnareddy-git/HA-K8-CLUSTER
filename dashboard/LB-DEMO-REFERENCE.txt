Q: How does Kubernetes detect pod failures?
A: Through liveness and readiness probes. The kubelet checks pods every 
   few seconds and removes unhealthy ones from service endpoints.

Q: How fast is the failover?
A: Typically 1-2 seconds after failure detection. The demo shows it 
   instantly for visualization.

Q: Can we control which algorithm is used?
A: Yes! With kube-proxy in IPVS mode, you can configure different 
   scheduling algorithms.

Q: What's the difference between ClusterIP and LoadBalancer?
A: ClusterIP is internal only. LoadBalancer creates an external LB 
   (cloud provider) with a public IP.

Q: Do all cloud providers support this?
A: Yes! AWS (ELB/ALB), GCP (Cloud Load Balancing), Azure (Load Balancer)
   all integrate with Kubernetes Services.
